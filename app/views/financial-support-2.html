Eligible for some financial support

You will have to pay a contribution to the cost of your care from your income, plus an extra amount depending on your capital

The extra amount of contributions you would pay is called a Tariff Amount. It applies at a rate of £1 for every £250 you have between £14,250 and £23,250, so could be between £1 and £36 per week.

For example, if you have £19,500 in capital:

£19,500 - £14,250 = £5,250

£5,250 / £250 = £21 per week

How do we work out how much your contribution will be?

We follow a simple 4 step process.

  1. Step 1 Add together all of your different income

    Many people only have one source of income, such as their State Pension, but if you have other sources of income we will take them into account.

    These may include:

    • tariff amount (depending on amount of capital beween £14,250 and £23,250)
    • state pension
    • wages/salary
    • private/work pensions
    • rental income from tenants and boarders
    • maintenance payments from a spouse/partner

    Income from benefits is usually included:

    • Attendance Allowance, including Constant Attendance Allowance and Exceptionally Severe Disablement Allowance
    • Bereavement Support Payment (has replaced Bereavement Allowance, previously Widow’s Pension)
    • Carer's Allowance
    • Child Tax Credit
    • Child Benefit
    • Disability Living Allowance (Care component)
    • Employment and Support Allowance, or the benefits this replaces such as Severe Disablement Allowance and Incapacity Benefit
    • Income Support
    • Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit or equivalent benefits
    • Jobseeker’s Allowance
    • Maternity Allowance
    • Pension Credit
    • Personal Independence Payment (Daily Living component)
    • Universal Credit
    • Working Tax Credit

    We may ask you to apply for benefits to which you are entitled but have not yet claimed, to reduce the burden on council budgets.

  2. Step 2 Add up any income we don't count

    There are some things we exclude, for example:

    • Pension credit (savings credit)
    • Attendance allowance
    • Income Support +25%
    • Disability related expenditure (Such as alarm services, disability equipment, extra heating costs, additional laundry/bedding and other costs)
    • Household Expenditure Costs (Such as, rent, mortgage, Council Tax, service charges, buildings insurance, water rates and sewerage, compulsory court payments)

  3. Step 3 Compare the assessed income with the total cost of care

    Your total income (1), less any of the things we exclude (2), is your Assessed Maximum Contribution.

    You will not be asked to pay more than your Assessed Maximum Contribution. If the cost of your care is less than your Assessed Maximum Contribution, you will pay the lower amount.

What happens next

If you are having, or have already had a Care Assessment and want to know exactly how much you will pay for services or you want to apply for financial support, you can use the BetterCare tool to complete a Financial Assessment.
