Eligible for some financial support

You will have to pay a contribution to the cost of your care from your income, plus an extra amount depending on your capital

How do we work out how much your contribution will be?

We follow a simple 4 step process.

Example

Mr. Jones is 88 and is currently residing in short term residential care at The Cottage Care Home. The cost of his service is £489.00 per week. He has the following capital and savings:

£19,500 - Natwest bank accounts

£19,500 - Total Capital

As Mr Jones has capital over £14,250, capital tariff of £1 for every £250 of capital over £14,250 will be included as part of his income, calculated as follows:

£19,500 - £14,250 = £5,250

£4,250 / £250 = £21.00.

Mr. Jones incurs capital tariff of £21.00 per week.

  1. Step 1 Add together all of your different income

    List the different sources of income. Here is an example:

    1. Income

    2. £125.00 per week - State Retirement Pension

    3. £17.00 per week - Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit)

    4. £3.20 per week - Pension Credit (Savings Credit)

    5. £21.00 per week - Capital Tariff (as calculated above)

    6. £166.20 per week - Total Weekly Income

  2. Step 2 Add up any income we don't count

    There are some things we exclude, for example:

    1. Discounts and allowances

    2. £24.90 per week - Personal Expenses Allowance

    3. £3.20 per week - Pension Credit (Savings Credit)

    4. £28.10 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

  3. Step 3 Calculate total income that can be used for contributions

    Take the total weekly discounts and allowances from the total weekly income.

    1. £166.20 per week - Total Weekly Income

    2. -£28.10 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

    3. £138.10 per week - Weekly contribution

  4. Step 4 Compare the assessed income with the total cost of care

    We then compare the total assessed income (£138.10 per week), with the total cost of services (£489.00 per week). Mr. Jones would pay whichever amount is lower.

    He will pay £138.10 per week toward the cost of his care.

What happens next

If you are having, or have already had a Care Assessment and want to know exactly how much you will pay for services or you want to apply for financial support, you can use the BetterCare tool to complete a Financial Assessment.
